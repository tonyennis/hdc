About Quilts
Knowing the underlined terms in particular will help the ordering process.
Your quilt will be made out of 15‚Äù squares called blocks. These will be cut from the shirts (or occasionally other fabrics) you provide. Each side of an adult-sized shirt can provide one block, though if a shirt is very small we may only be able to get one block out of it. Shirts of any material can be used; they do not have to be cotton.
Sashing is the fabric between blocks on certain quilts. This is an optional step and can make a t-shirt quilt look more formal or fancy. Sashing strips are about an inch and a half wide each, and thus add to both the length and width of the quilt.
A quilt consists of three parts:
  1. The quilt top. This is pieced together from blocks and sashing (if chosen).
  2. The batting, which is the fluffy insulation in between the quilt top and backing.
  3. The backing, which is the fabric on the back of the quilt. This will be a solid color fabric of your choosing. If the quilt is sashed, this should generally match the sashing fabric.
Please click on the "samples" button on the toolbar to see some examples of quilts of different sizes, with and without sashing.
When you are ready, click "order" on the taskbar to get started on your quilt! You can either build and order your t-shirt quilt online, or have us send you our step-by-step ordering kit.
